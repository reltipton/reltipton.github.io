Obesity in the United States continues to be a leading health concern because of its relationship
to cardiovascular disease, hypertension, and diabetes. Even with all the information available on
how to eat healthily, the “American Diet” continues to center around an abundance of meat, sugar,
and fast food. We are in a health crisis that shows no signs of slowing down as poor eating habits
are passed down from generation to generation. The team looked at trends of obesity in the
United States and the relationship between this health epidemic and the growing number of fast
food restaurants across the country.

Together the two maps highlight how the increase of fast food restaurants has affected the
nation resulting in only 7 of the 50 states having normal BMI percentages. This observation
led us to take a deeper look at the population accounting for these high rates. We
broke the population up by gender to understand the prevalence of obesity and class 3 (morbidly) obesity.
Our analysis consisted of performing a year over year percentage difference for 2006 – 2014.
You can see that there was a drop overall in 2012, but a drastic spike in 2014 and an upward trend
overall. When looking at the total percentage of obese population you can see an upward trend
from 2006 – 2014 and an astounding 76% of the population obese in 2014 and 16% morbidly obese.

make better choices we can help reverse this epidemic. With this being said reviewed
the content, sentiment and emotional valence of eleven TED Talks featuring buzzwords
regarding obesity trends and the prevalence of fast food restaurants.

Obesity is an emotional topic for many Americans as it impacts so many fragile communities
across the country. TED talks that included words related to the sentiments of anger,
surprise, and trust had a greater emotional connection with audience.

Case for Change



After reviewing the above visualizations, it is obvious that obesity in the United States
is, in fact, a growing problem. Government officials and influencers need to take a stand
today. This can be done through powerful speeches or social media posts to help the public
understand the issues surrounding fast food and obesity in the United States. In order to
elicit the emotions of anticipation, joy, and fear that are essential in getting the public
to listen, buzzwords such as “think, know, food, people and now” need to be used in communications.
Without intervention, fast food presence will continue to flourish and obesity in the US
will increase in correlation.
